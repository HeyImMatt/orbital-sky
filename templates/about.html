About the Project

What's Up Spock! is the brain child of developers Matt McFarland, Alan Puglisi, and James Reid. Created for NASA's International Space App Challenge, What's Up Spock is the ultimate tool for tracking your local satellite activity. Simply click the button and your location will be cross referenced with multiple databases of international satellites.

You'll receive an up-to-date visual representation of the satellites orbiting your area. Filter down even further to see your local weather, GIS, or Military satellites. You'll be provided with a panoply of data about these satellites. Their launch date, country of origin, and (if available) a detailed history from Wikipedia.

Ever wonder what those strange blinking, lights in the sky are? Ever wondered which satellites predict your weather or provide your television? Are you paranoid about the 2,666 satellites currently orbiting Earth? Well, give What's Up Spock! a spin and put those curiosities to rest.
